THE 30-DAY
FOLLOW-UP SYSTEM

6 strategic touchpoints with scripts for calls, texts, voicemails, and emails - from first contact to close.

The 4 Phases of Follow-Up

Phase 1
Days 1-2
Speed & Urgency

Respond fast. Set the tone. Establish contact before the competition does.

Phase 2
Day 5
Value Reinforcement

Bring value with every touch. Updates, pricing, new inventory - give them a reason.

Phase 3
Day 12
Pattern Interruption

Change the approach. Ask different questions. Break through the noise.

Phase 4
Days 25, 30
Professional Persistence

Close the loop professionally. Either book the appointment or close the file.

Why 6 Touchpoints Instead of 28

Most follow-up systems burn out both the salesperson and the customer. Calling every single day for a month isn't persistence - it's noise. Here's the difference:

Fewer touches, more impact

Each touchpoint has a specific purpose and multiple script options. You're not just "checking in" - you have real reasons to reach out.

Strategic silence works

The gaps between touchpoints let the customer feel space, not pressure. When you do reach out, it means something.

Multi-channel every time

Instead of one text on Day 6 and one call on Day 7, each touchpoint gives you call, text, voicemail, and email options.

Actually sustainable

6 touchpoints per customer means you can follow this system for every lead, not just the ones you remember.

Phase 1

Speed & Urgency

Days 1-2
DAY
1

First Contact

The goal: make contact within 5 minutes. Speed wins deals. Hit them across every channel.

CALL
"Hey [Name], this is Tyler at Garber Chevrolet Midland. You inquired about the [Vehicle]. I wanted to see what stood out to you about it."

Why this works: Open-ended question that gets them talking about what they want - not a yes/no that lets them dismiss you.

VOICEMAIL
"Hi [Name], Tyler at Garber Chevrolet Midland. I'm calling about the [Vehicle] you looked at. I'll send you a quick text as well."

Key: Under 20 seconds. Name, reason, next step. Then hang up - don't ramble.

TEXT
"Hey [Name], it's Tyler at Garber Chevrolet Midland. Just tried you regarding the [Vehicle]. When's a good time to connect?"

Why this works: Short, personal, ends with a question. Gives them an easy way to respond.

EMAIL
"Hi [Name], thanks for your interest in the [Vehicle]. I'm Tyler at Garber Chevrolet Midland. I have this vehicle on the lot right now. What's the best way to reach you - call or text?"
DAY
2

Same-Day Follow-Up

They didn't respond yesterday. Call again - most reps stop here. Don't be most reps.

CALL
"Hey [Name], Tyler again at Garber Chevrolet Midland. Following up on your inquiry about the [Vehicle]. Did you have a chance to look at the details I sent over?"

Why this works: References yesterday's contact. Shows consistency without being pushy.

TEXT
"[Name], the [Vehicle] you looked at is getting a lot of attention. Wanted to give you first shot before we push it out more."
Days 3-4: No Contact

Give them breathing room. Two days of silence after two days of contact makes your next touchpoint feel intentional, not desperate.

Phase 2

Value Reinforcement

Day 5
DAY
5

Pricing Update

Bring real value - not "just checking in." New information earns the right to call.

CALL
"Hey [Name], quick update - we just adjusted pricing on a few models similar to what you looked at."

Why this works: You're not asking for anything. You're giving information. People respond to value, not pressure.

TEXT
"Hey [Name], heads up - some pricing adjustments just came through on models like the one you were looking at. Worth a look."
EMAIL
"Hi [Name], I wanted to let you know we just got some pricing adjustments on models like the [Vehicle]. I put together some additional details including current incentives. If you'd like me to send those over, just reply or give me a call."
Days 6-11: No Contact

A full week of silence. If they're interested, this gap gives them space to come to you. If they're not, the next touchpoint will use a completely different approach.

Phase 3

Pattern Interruption

Day 12
DAY
12

Pattern Interrupt

Stop selling. Start asking. A direct question forces a decision - not another "let me think about it."

CALL
"Hey [Name], quick question - are you still considering upgrading your vehicle this month?"

Why this works: Forces a yes or no. If yes, you re-engage. If no, you find out why and adjust. Either way, you get real information.

TEXT
"Hey [Name], still thinking about that upgrade? Just curious where you're at."
Days 13-24: No Contact

Nearly two weeks of silence. This is the hardest part for most salespeople. Trust the process. The final two touchpoints are your strongest plays.

Phase 4

Professional Persistence

Days 25, 30
DAY
25

The "Close or Continue" Call

The most important call of the entire sequence. Give them an out - and watch how many come back.

CALL
"Hey [Name], I haven't heard back and I don't want to over-contact you. Should I close your file or is timing just not right yet?"

Why this works: This is the "breakup" script. Psychologically, people don't want to lose options. Many will re-engage when they think the door is closing.

TEXT
"Hey [Name], last reach-out for now. If you ever want to pick this back up, I'm one text away."
EMAIL
"Hi [Name], I've reached out a few times and I want to respect your time. I'm going to close your file, but before I do - if there's anything holding you back, I'd be happy to help sort it out. If the timing just isn't right, no hard feelings. You've got my number whenever you're ready."
DAY
30

Final Professional Close

The last touch. Professional, clean, and door left open.

CALL
"Hey [Name], Tyler at Garber Chevrolet Midland. I know I've reached out a few times. Just want to respect your time - is this still something you're considering, or should I circle back later?"
TEXT
"Hey [Name], it was great connecting with you. I'm closing out your file for now, but if you ever need anything car-related - pricing, trade value, service - don't hesitate to reach out. Talk soon."

Why this ends well: Professional. No guilt. The door stays open. Many customers come back weeks or months later because you treated them with respect.

The Rules

01
Speed Wins

The first 5 minutes after an inquiry are worth more than the next 5 days.

02
Never "Just Check In"

Every touch needs a reason. Pricing, inventory, incentives - bring value or don't call.

03
Multi-Channel Always

Call + voicemail + text + email. Multiple channels per touchpoint maximizes your contact rate.

04
Strategic Silence

The gaps between touchpoints are intentional. Silence makes your next contact feel purposeful, not desperate.
