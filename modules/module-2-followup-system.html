THE 30-DAY
FOLLOW-UP SYSTEM

A complete day-by-day roadmap with scripts for calls, voicemails, and texts — from first contact to close.

The 4 Phases of Follow-Up

Phase 1
Days 1–3
Speed & Urgency

Respond fast. Set the tone. Establish contact before the competition does.

Phase 2
Days 4–10
Value Reinforcement

Bring value with every touch. Updates, pricing, new inventory — give them a reason.

Phase 3
Days 11–20
Pattern Interruption

Change the approach. Ask different questions. Break through the noise.

Phase 4
Days 21–30
Professional Persistence

Close the loop professionally. Either book the appointment or close the file.

Phase 1

Speed & Urgency

Days 1–3
DAY
1

First Contact — Internet Lead Example

The goal: make contact within 5 minutes. Speed wins deals.

📞 CALL
"Hey [Name], this is Tyler at [Dealership]. You inquired about the [Vehicle]. I wanted to see what stood out to you about it."

Why this works: Open-ended question that gets them talking about what they want — not a yes/no that lets them dismiss you.

💬 IF NO ANSWER — VOICEMAIL
"Hi [Name], Tyler at [Dealership]. I'm calling about the [Vehicle] you looked at. I'll send you a quick text as well."

Key: Under 20 seconds. Name, reason, next step. Then hang up — don't ramble.

📱 TEXT
"Hey [Name], it's Tyler at [Dealership]. Just tried you regarding the [Vehicle]. When's a good time to connect?"

Why this works: Short, personal, ends with a question. Gives them an easy way to respond.

DAY
2

Same-Day Follow-Up

They didn't respond yesterday. Call again — most reps stop here. Don't be most reps.

📞 CALL
"Hey [Name], Tyler again at [Dealership]. I reached out yesterday about the [Vehicle]. I've got some availability today if you'd like to come see it in person — does this afternoon or tomorrow morning work better?"

Why this works: Assumptive close with two time options. Not "do you want to come in?" but "which time works?"

📱 TEXT
"Hey [Name], just checking in — the [Vehicle] is still available. I can have it pulled up and ready for you. What time works today?"
DAY
3

Urgency Touch

Create urgency without being pushy. Real information creates real urgency.

📞 CALL
"Hey [Name], Tyler at [Dealership]. Quick heads-up — we've had a couple of other inquiries on the [Vehicle]. I wanted to give you first shot before it goes. Can you swing by today or tomorrow?"

Why this works: Scarcity is real in automotive. If the vehicle has interest, tell them. Honest urgency converts.

📱 TEXT
"Hey [Name], wanted to let you know we've had some interest on the [Vehicle]. I'd hate for you to miss out — want me to hold it for you?"
Phase 2

Value Reinforcement

Days 4–10
DAY
5

Pricing Update

Bring real value — not "just checking in." New information earns the right to call.

📞 CALL
"Hey [Name], quick update — we just adjusted pricing on a few models similar to what you looked at."

Why this works: You're not asking for anything. You're giving information. People respond to value, not pressure.

📱 TEXT
"Hey [Name], some new pricing just came through on the [Vehicle] and similar models. Want me to send you the updated numbers?"
DAY
7

Alternative Vehicle

Maybe their first choice wasn't perfect. Offer an alternative that might fit better.

📞 CALL
"Hey [Name], Tyler at [Dealership]. We just got a [Similar Vehicle] in that I thought you might like — similar to what you were looking at but with [key difference]. Worth a look?"

Why this works: Shows you're paying attention and actively working for them. This is sales as a service.

📱 TEXT
"Hey [Name], we just got something in I thought of you — a [Similar Vehicle]. Want me to send pics?"
DAY
10

Value Recap

Summarize the value you've offered. Transition from "push" to "pull."

📞 CALL
"Hey [Name], Tyler here. I've sent over some options and updates over the last week. I don't want to be a pest — just want to make sure you have everything you need to make a good decision. Anything I can help with?"

Why this works: Self-aware and respectful. Positions you as a resource, not a nuisance.

Phase 3

Pattern Interruption

Days 11–20
DAY
12

Pattern Interrupt

Stop selling. Start asking. A direct question forces a decision — not another "let me think about it."

📞 CALL
"Hey [Name], quick question — are you still considering upgrading your vehicle this month?"

Why this works: Forces a yes or no. If yes, you re-engage. If no, you find out why and adjust. Either way, you get real information.

📱 TEXT
"Hey [Name], quick one — still in the market or have you put it on hold?"
DAY
15

The "Honest Check-In"

Transparent and human. Drop the salesperson act.

📞 CALL
"Hey [Name], I'll be straight with you — I know you've been getting my messages. I'm not trying to pressure you. I just want to know if I should keep looking for options for you or if you've gone in a different direction."

Why this works: Radical honesty disarms people. They expect pressure — you give them respect. That's memorable.

DAY
18

The Third-Party Approach

Use social proof. People trust what other people are doing.

📱 TEXT
"Hey [Name], had a customer come in yesterday looking at the same [Vehicle] you liked. Reminded me of you — is that something you're still interested in?"

Why this works: Subtle social proof. Someone else wants what they want. Creates urgency without direct pressure.

Phase 4

Professional Persistence

Days 21–30
DAY
22

Last Value Touch

One more piece of genuine value before the closing sequence.

📞 CALL
"Hey [Name], Tyler here. I know it's been a few weeks — just wanted to let you know we have some month-end incentives coming up that could save you some money on the [Vehicle]. Worth a conversation?"

Why this works: Month-end incentives are real and time-limited. Genuine value with natural urgency.

DAY
25

The "Close or Continue" Call

The most important call of the entire sequence. Give them an out — and watch how many come back.

📞 CALL
"Hey [Name], I haven't heard back and I don't want to over-contact you. Should I close your file or is timing just not right yet?"

Why this works: This is the "breakup" script. Psychologically, people don't want to lose options. Many will re-engage when they think the door is closing.

📱 TEXT
"Hey [Name], I'm going to close your file unless I hear back. No hard feelings either way — just don't want to keep bugging you. Should I keep you on my list or close it out?"
DAY
30

Final Professional Close

The last touch. Professional, clean, and door left open.

📱 TEXT
"Hey [Name], Tyler here. I'm closing out your file today. If anything changes down the road, you've got my number. I'll always take care of you. Good luck with everything!"

Why this ends well: Professional. No guilt. The door stays open. Many customers come back weeks or months later because you treated them with respect.

The Rules

01
Speed Wins

The first 5 minutes after an inquiry are worth more than the next 5 days.

02
Never "Just Check In"

Every touch needs a reason. Pricing, inventory, incentives — bring value or don't call.

03
Multi-Channel Always

Call + voicemail + text. Three touches in one attempt triples your contact rate.

04
Close the File Professionally

The breakup script works. Give them an out and many will come back.
