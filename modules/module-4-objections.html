Objection Handling Library

Master the 4-Step Formula to turn every objection into an appointment. Stop getting stuck — start redirecting.

Objective: Learn the 4-step objection handling formula and apply it to the most common customer objections with ready-to-use scripts.

The 4-Step Formula

Most objections follow the same pattern. Learn the four steps and you'll know what to say.

1
Step 1

Acknowledge

Validate their concern. Show you heard them. No arguing, no defending.

→
2
Step 2

Clarify

Ask a question to find the real issue underneath. Most objections hide the actual concern.

→
3
Step 3

Reframe

Shift the perspective. Give them a new way to look at the situation that works in their favor.

→
4
Step 4

Close

Redirect back to the appointment. Every response should end with a next step.

Common Objections & Scripts

Customer Says

“I’m just looking.”

Your Response

“I understand. Most customers start there. Just so I can help properly, are you comparing models or just exploring pricing?”

Step Breakdown
1
Acknowledge

“I understand.”

2
Clarify

“Most customers start there.” — normalizes their position

3
Reframe

“Just so I can help properly” — positions you as a guide, not a pusher

4
Close

“Are you comparing models or just exploring pricing?” — gives them two paths, both lead forward

Customer Says

“I need to think about it.”

Your Response

“Of course. Usually when someone says that, there’s one specific thing they’re unsure about. Is it the payment, the vehicle, or the timing?”

Step Breakdown
1
Acknowledge

“Of course.” — no pressure, no judgment

2
Clarify

“Usually there’s one specific thing” — narrows the vague objection into something real

3
Reframe

Turns “thinking” into a solvable, specific concern

4
Close

“Is it payment, vehicle, or timing?” — three options, all answerable right now

Customer Says

“The payment is too high.”

Your Response

“If we could adjust the structure to stay closer to your target, would everything else make sense?”

Step Breakdown
1
Acknowledge

Implied — you don’t argue the number, you work with it

2
Clarify

“Closer to your target” — confirms what they actually want to pay

3
Reframe

“Adjust the structure” — shows flexibility without giving away profit

4
Close

“Would everything else make sense?” — isolates payment as the only barrier

Customer Says

“I’m shopping other dealers.”

Your Response

“That makes sense. While you’re comparing, what’s most important — price, vehicle availability, or the service experience?”

Step Breakdown
1
Acknowledge

“That makes sense.” — validates their smart buyer behavior

2
Clarify

“What’s most important?” — discovers their real buying criteria

3
Reframe

“While you’re comparing” — joins their process instead of fighting it

4
Close

“Price, availability, or service?” — three options, you can win on any of them

The Point

An objection means they're still talking to you. That's a good thing. The worst thing you can do is argue. The best thing you can do is acknowledge, clarify, reframe, and redirect. Every objection is a doorway to the appointment.
