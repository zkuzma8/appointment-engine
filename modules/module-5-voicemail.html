VOICEMAIL SYSTEM:
THE 20-SECOND FRAMEWORK

Objective: Leave voicemails that get callbacks.

"Most voicemails fail because they're too long, too vague, or too desperate.

The fix is simple: 20 seconds. 4 parts. Every time.

If you can't say it in 20 seconds, you're saying too much."

The 20-Second Framework

20 seconds
1

Name

Who you are. First name, dealership. Nothing else.

~3 seconds

2

Reason

Why you're calling. One sentence. Be specific.

~5 seconds

3

Call to Action

What they should do next. Give your number. Keep it easy.

~7 seconds

4

Stop Talking

End it. Don't ramble. Don't repeat. Just stop.

~5 seconds

Example Voicemail

Voicemail Script

"Hi [Name], Tyler at Garber Chevrolet Midland. Calling about your vehicle inquiry. When you get a moment, give me a call at [number]. I'll keep it quick."

Breakdown: Which Part Is Which

1
Name

"Hi [Name], Tyler at Garber Chevrolet Midland."

2
Reason

"Calling about your vehicle inquiry."

3
Call to Action

"When you get a moment, give me a call at [number]."

4
Stop Talking

"I'll keep it quick." - Done. Hang up.

Common Mistakes

✗

Too long (over 20 seconds)

If they wanted a long conversation, they would have answered the phone. Keep it tight.

✗

No clear call to action

If you don't tell them what to do, they won't do anything. Always give a next step.

✗

Rambling or repeating information

Say it once. Don't repeat your name, number, or reason. Once is enough.

✗

Sounding desperate or overly salesy

You're offering value, not begging. Calm, confident, professional. That's it.

✗

Leaving your phone number too fast

Slow down on the digits. If they have to replay the message to catch your number, most won't bother.

What Helps

✓

Smile while you talk

It sounds crazy, but smiling changes your tone. People can hear it. You'll sound warmer and more approachable.

✓

Stand up for more energy

Standing opens your diaphragm and adds natural energy to your voice. Sit down = flat delivery.

✓

Practice until it's natural, not scripted

Read it 10 times. Then say it without reading. It should feel like a conversation, not a performance.

✓

Always leave a voicemail

Even if they never call back, it builds familiarity. When you finally connect, they'll recognize your name.

Summary

  • 1 Every voicemail has exactly 4 parts: Name, Reason, Call to Action, Stop Talking.
  • 2 20 seconds max. If you can't say it in 20 seconds, you're saying too much.
  • 3 Always leave a voicemail. It builds familiarity even if they don't call back.
  • 4 Sound confident, not desperate. You're offering value, not begging for attention.
